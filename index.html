SO HOW DO WE REACH OUR AUDIENCE?

Effective
Engagement

Crafting the consumer funnel for a B2B digital agency involves mapping out the journey that their business clients take from initial awareness to becoming loyal advocates.


It begins with industry engagement, where they aim to establish credibility and thought leadership through activities like sharing insights, hosting webinars, and networking with industry leaders. As potential clients move into the research phase, the focus should be on providing valuable content and resources to help them explore their options and understand how the services offered can meet their needs. When clients are seriously considering working with the agency, the focus shifts to showcasing previous work transparently, sharing success stories, and building trust. Finally, after clients have partnered with the agency, the emphasis is on providing exceptional service, smooth onboarding, and ongoing support to foster long-term relationships and encourage referrals. Below is an illustration of how this funnel might look for a B2B digital agency.


Additionally, effective marketing is not one-size-fits-all. It's about connecting with your audience on a personal level, understanding their unique needs and preferences, and tailoring your approach accordingly. This is where consumer personas come in. By identifying and empathizing with different segments of your audience, you can create targeted strategies that resonate with their needs and preferences, ultimately driving engagement and loyalty. Below are three consumer personas that August can leverage to reach their target audience effectively.

SME Owner Sarah:

Background: Sarah is the owner of a small to medium-sized enterprise (SME), specializing in handmade artisanal products.

Goals: She aims to expand her business's online presence, increase sales, and reach a wider audience.

Challenges: Limited resources and expertise in digital marketing and e-commerce platforms.

Needs: Sarah needs a digital agency that offers comprehensive services such as website development, SEO optimization, social media management, and e-commerce solutions tailored to her budget and business goals.

Marketing Manager Max:

Background: Max works as a marketing manager for a mid-sized technology company specializing in software solutions.

Goals: He is focused on increasing brand visibility, generating leads, and driving customer engagement through digital channels.

Challenges: Balancing multiple marketing initiatives, staying updated on digital trends, and demonstrating ROI to company stakeholders.

Needs: Max requires a digital agency partner that can provide strategic guidance, execution support, and data-driven insights across various digital marketing channels, including content marketing, PPC advertising, email campaigns, and conversion optimization.

Startup Founder Mia:

Background: Mia is the founder of a tech startup developing a mobile app for fitness enthusiasts.

Goals: She aims to launch her product successfully, acquire early adopters, and secure funding from investors.

Challenges: Limited budget, fierce competition in the app market, and the need to differentiate her brand and product offering.

Needs: Mia is looking for a digital agency that specializes in startup growth strategies, including branding, user experience design, app development, influencer marketing, and user acquisition tactics tailored to the startup ecosystem and her target audience.

INTERESTING, BUT HOW DO WE PUT THIS INTO ACTION?

The Strategy

Let's break down the strategy into actionable steps that August can take to enhance its digital marketing efforts and achieve its business objectives. First, we'll explore each phase of the consumer funnel and identify key strategies for engaging with potential clients at different stages of their journey. We will also break down the Industry Awareness stage further into Problem and Solution Awareness to understand the nuances of buyer intent and content relevance.

1. Problem Aware

Targeting:

  • Target businesses in industries or segments facing common challenges related to digital marketing, web development, or online presence.

  • Focus on businesses experiencing issues such as low website traffic, poor conversion rates, outdated websites, or ineffective digital marketing strategies.

Messaging Pillars:

  • Acknowledge and empathize with the specific pain points and challenges faced by the target audience.

  • Highlight the negative impact of unresolved problems on business growth, customer acquisition, and competitive positioning.

  • Position the agency as a trusted advisor and resource for businesses seeking solutions to their digital challenges.

Structural Pillars:

  • Develop educational blog posts, articles, or guides addressing common problems and providing actionable insights and solutions.

  • Create dedicated landing pages or website sections focused on addressing specific pain points and challenges identified during the problem-aware stage.

  • Implement SEO strategies to optimize content for relevant keywords and ensure visibility to businesses searching for solutions to their problems.

Implementation:

  • Develop blog posts addressing pain points and challenges of a persona. Example; for SME Owner Sarah, titles include "Unlocking Growth: Overcoming Digital Marketing Challenges for Small Businesses" targeting keywords like "digital marketing challenges for small businesses" and "how to improve small business online growth." These posts will emphasize the negative impact of issues like low website traffic and ineffective digital marketing strategies.

  • We'll also incorporate data figures, such as "70% of SMEs cite limited budget constraints as their biggest digital marketing challenge," to add credibility and engagement.

  • Add interactive elements such as adding question prompts like "Struggling with low website traffic?" and "Facing challenges with having an online presence?" to encourage user engagement and drive action.

  • Implement a lead capture form or call-to-action (CTA) at the end of the blog post to capture inquiries and move prospects further down the funnel.

  • Create Youtube content with tutorial videos on topics such as "SEO Optimization for small businesses" and "Digital Marketing Strategies on a budget".

KPIs
  1. Blog Posts:

    • Website Traffic: Measure the increase in website traffic, particularly to blog post pages, using tools like Google Analytics.

    • Engagement Metrics: Track metrics such as time spent on page, bounce rate, and scroll depth to gauge reader engagement with blog content.

    • Lead Generation: Monitor the number of leads generated through lead capture forms or CTAs embedded within blog posts.

    • Keyword Rankings: Monitor the ranking positions of targeted keywords in search engine results pages (SERPs) to assess SEO performance.

    • Social Shares: Track the number of social shares for each blog post to gauge its virality and reach.

  2. YouTube Content:

    • View Count: Track the number of views for each tutorial video to measure its reach and popularity.

    • Engagement Metrics: Monitor metrics like likes, comments, and shares to gauge viewer engagement and interaction with the content.

    • Subscriber Growth: Measure the increase in subscribers to the YouTube channel as a result of publishing tutorial videos.

  3. Lead Capture Forms/CTAs:

    • Conversion Rate: Measure the percentage of visitors who complete the lead capture form or click on the CTA to become leads.

    • Lead Quality: Assess the quality of leads generated through lead capture forms by tracking metrics such as lead source, demographics, and behavior.

2. Solution Aware

Targeting:

  • Target businesses that have demonstrated interest in digital solutions and are actively seeking information on potential providers or agencies.

  • Focus on businesses that have engaged with problem-aware content or have shown intent through online behavior such as visiting relevant websites or engaging with related social media content.

Messaging Pillars:

  • Introduce the agency's services and solutions as potential answers to the problems and challenges identified in the problem-aware stage.

  • Emphasize the unique value proposition of the agency and how its offerings can address specific pain points and deliver tangible results.

  • Highlight success stories, case studies, or testimonials showcasing the agency's track record of solving similar problems for other businesses.

Structural Pillars:

  • Develop targeted content assets such as solution-focused blog posts, case studies, or webinars highlighting the agency's expertise and approach to solving specific challenges.

  • Create landing pages or service pages on the website dedicated to showcasing the agency's solutions and offerings in a clear and compelling manner.

  • Implement lead capture forms or CTAs on solution-focused content to capture inquiries and move prospects further down the sales funnel.

Implementation:

  • Develop solution-focused blog posts targeting businesses in the Solution Aware phase. Example titles include "Unlocking Business Growth: Our Proven Digital Solutions" targeting keywords like "digital solutions for businesses" and "effective strategies for online growth." These posts will introduce the agency's services, emphasizing their unique value proposition and success stories through case studies and testimonials.

  • Incorporate data figures, such as "80% of businesses experience a boost in ROI after implementing these digital solutions,"

  • Create YouTube tutorial videos on topics like "Optimizing Your Online Presence: Digital Solutions for Business Success" and "Maximizing ROI: Effective Digital Marketing Strategies," providing actionable insights to businesses seeking solutions.

KPIs

  • The KPIs would be similar to the Problem Aware phase, focusing on metrics such as website traffic, engagement, lead generation, keyword rankings, social shares, view count, engagement metrics, subscriber growth, and conversion rate.

3. Brand Awareness:

Targeting:

  • Target businesses in relevant industries or segments through organic channels such as content marketing, SEO, social media, and industry partnerships.

Messaging Pillars:

  • Educate potential clients about industry trends, best practices, and emerging technologies related to digital marketing and web development.

  • Position the agency as a thought leader and trusted advisor by providing valuable insights, tips, and resources.

Structural Pillars:

  • Develop a content strategy focused on creating informative blog posts, articles, infographics, and videos that address common challenges and interests within the target audience.

  • Leverage social media platforms, email newsletters, and industry forums to distribute content and engage with potential clients.

Implementation:

  • Content Creation: Develop a series of blog posts, articles, infographics, and videos focusing on industry trends, best practices, and emerging technologies in digital marketing and web development. For example, create a blog post titled "Top 10 Digital Marketing Trends for 2024" and a video tutorial on "Mastering Social Media Marketing: Tips for Small Businesses".

  • Engagement Strategies: Actively engage with potential clients by responding to comments and inquiries on social media, hosting Q&A sessions or webinars, and participating in industry-related discussions. For example, respond to comments on blog posts, host a live webinar on "Effective Email Marketing Strategies", and participate in LinkedIn chats related to digital marketing.

  • Sponsorship Opportunities: Explore opportunities to sponsor workshops, seminars, or industry events relevant to the target audience. This could involve collaborating with industry organizations or participating in relevant conferences and events. For instance, sponsor a digital marketing workshop for small businesses in collaboration with a local chamber of commerce or participate as a speaker at a technology conference discussing the future of web development.

KPIs
  1. Content Engagement and Conversion metrics:

    • These are similar to the Problem Aware and Solution Aware phases, focusing on metrics such as website traffic, engagement, lead generation, keyword rankings, social shares, view count, engagement metrics, subscriber growth, and conversion rate.

  2. Audience Interaction and Engagement:

    • Track the number of comments, inquiries, and interactions on social media posts, blog articles, and webinars to gauge audience engagement and interest.

    • Measure the number of attendees and participant engagement during webinars or Q&A sessions hosted by the agency.

  3. Brand Visibility and Reach

    • Monitor brand mentions and discussions on social media, industry forums, and other online platforms to assess brand visibility and reputation.

    • Track the reach and impressions of social media posts to evaluate brand visibility and audience reach.

  4. Partnership and Collaboration Metrics

    • Evaluate the number of partnership opportunities identified and pursued with industry organizations, influencers, or complementary businesses.

    • Measure the effectiveness of event sponsorships or participation by assessing attendee engagement, lead generation, and brand exposure.

6. Conversion/Partnership Formed:

Messaging Pillars:

  • Personalize communication and outreach efforts based on the prospect's previous interactions, interests, and needs identified during the solution-aware stage.

  • Provide tailored solutions, proposals, or recommendations that address the specific pain points and objectives of the prospect's business.

  • Highlight the benefits of partnering with the agency, such as expertise, reliability, and a track record of delivering results.

Structural Pillars:

  • Implement lead nurturing workflows and email sequences to provide ongoing value, address objections, and guide prospects towards conversion.

  • Develop customized proposals, presentations, or consultations tailored to the prospect's needs and objectives.
